DAC Guide

This guide document attempts at providing a generic overview of General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)-related concepts that you as a Data Access Committee (DAC) member to a dataset in FEGA Norway need to be aware of in relation to function/policies in your own institution and the GDPR.

Reviewing requests for data access to a dataset submitted in FEGA Norway

  • A researcher initiates contact with the DAC main contact person for your dataset
  • Negotiate a Data Access Agreement (DAA)
    • Often in the form of a Data Transfer Agreement, where the requester takes on an independent Data Controller responsibility.
    • Other alternatives are specific Data Processing Agreements or joint Data Controller Agreement, but these require detailed clarifications and regulations up front.
    • FEGA Norway has developed a generic Data Access Agreement of different types:
      • DTA-based template
      • DPA-based template
  • In the event of an approved data access request after a signed DAA, your institution would normally require you to file a notification about
    • The signed DAA and a copy of it
    • Additional considerations regarding the processing of the request, including the requestors ability to secure the data and processing in compliance with the regulations of the dataset.
    • This should be filed through standard mechanisms for notification on projects containing sensitive data in your organisation.
  • fega-norway-support@elixir.no
